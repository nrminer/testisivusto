Signs it's time to stop

Any one of these is enough reason to step away from gambling — temporarily or for good.

  • You're playing with money that's meant for something else (rent, food, bills, someone else's needs).
  • You're hiding how much you play, or how much you've lost, from people you love.
  • You're chasing losses — betting bigger to get back to even.
  • You're gambling to feel better when you're low, not because it's fun.
  • You've tried to stop before and couldn't.
  • You're thinking about gambling when you're trying to do something else.
  • Someone close to you has told you it's becoming a problem.

None of this makes you a bad person. It makes you a human being with a brain that responds to intermittent reinforcement the way human brains do. Help exists because this is common.

International helplines

  • GamCare (UK, English, 24/7): gamcare.org.uk · 0808 8020 133. Free advice, live chat, and counselling. Available regardless of country.
  • BeGambleAware (UK, English): begambleaware.org · 0808 8020 133. Information, self-assessment tools, and treatment routes.
  • Gambling Therapy (international, multi-language): gamblingtherapy.org. Online counselling in multiple languages for anyone, anywhere.
  • GamAnon (family support, international): gam-anon.org. For family members and friends of people with gambling problems.

Self-exclusion schemes

Self-exclusion means asking licensed casinos and betting sites to refuse you service for a fixed period. It's enforced by the casino, not you — once active, you can't just log in again. Different countries run different schemes.

  • GAMSTOP (United Kingdom): gamstop.co.uk. Blocks access to all UKGC-licensed gambling sites for 6 months, 1 year, or 5 years. Free.
  • Spelpaus (Sweden): spelpaus.se. All Swedish-licensed gambling sites, 1–12 months or "until further notice." Free.
  • ROFUS (Denmark): rofus.nu. Danish-licensed operators, 1 month to permanent.
  • CRUKS (Netherlands): cruks.nl. Dutch-licensed operators, 6-month minimum.
  • OASIS (Germany): Contact your national gambling regulator or visit gluecksspiel-behoerde.de for the current registration route.
  • Finland: Self-exclusion tools at peliportti.fi and via Peluuri (peluuri.fi). Veikkaus also runs its own system.

Practical tools

Even if you're not ready for full self-exclusion, these tools help keep things in check:

  • Deposit limits. Set a weekly or monthly deposit cap in your casino account. Most casinos require a cooling-off period before limits can be raised again, which is the point.
  • Session limits. Set a maximum session length. Most casinos will log you out automatically when you hit it.
  • Loss limits. Stop play automatically when losses hit a defined amount.
  • Reality checks. Popup every 30/60 minutes reminding you how long you've been playing and how much you've spent.
  • Cooling-off periods. Temporary account freeze — 24 hours, a week, a month. Use when you feel a bad session coming on.
  • Blocking software. Gamban blocks gambling sites across all your devices for a set period — harder to circumvent than browser blocks.

If someone you love has a gambling problem

Gambling harm affects families and partners even more than individual finances. GamAnon is specifically for people in your position. Both GamCare and BeGambleAware also offer resources for family members, including advice on how to have difficult conversations and how to protect shared finances without shaming.

One practical note: don't cover gambling debts. It feels like help — it isn't. It removes the consequence that would otherwise drive the decision to stop.
