Affiliate disclosure

What you need to know about how this site makes money, what that does and doesn't change, and what a "Visit casino →" button actually does when you click it.

In one sentence

PappaPelailee.com earns a commission from casinos when you sign up to them using links on this site, and that commission does not change the scores or reviews.

What "affiliate link" means

Every "Visit casino →" button on this site is an affiliate link. When you click one, a tracking tag is attached to the URL so the casino knows you came from PappaPelailee.com. If you then register and deposit, the casino pays this site a commission — either a one-time cost-per-acquisition fee, a percentage of their revenue from your play (revenue share), or a mix of both. Every affiliate link on the site carries rel="sponsored nofollow" in line with Google's guidance on affiliate content.

What this costs you

Nothing. The commission is paid by the casino out of their marketing budget. Your deposit, your bonus, your wagering terms, and your payouts are identical whether you sign up through this site or directly. Affiliate links don't change the offer.

What this does not influence

  • Rating scores. Every casino is scored using the published seven-criterion rubric, and commission rates are not a criterion. Sub-scores and the final number are calculated before anyone looks at how the commercial deal is structured.
  • Review content. Cons, flaws, and fine-print traps are published whether or not they hurt signup rates. If a casino has a 40× D+B wagering requirement with a €500 cap, that's what the review says.
  • Order of the listing. The casino list is sorted by score by default — highest first. It is not sorted by commission rate, and the option to reorder it is available to readers (by wager, min deposit, newest review, A–Z).

What this does influence (honest list)

  • Which casinos get written about. Writing a full review takes days. Casinos with active affiliate programs are more likely to be reviewed first because that's how the site pays for itself. A casino without an affiliate program might not get a review at all, not because it's bad but because there's no economic case to research it.
  • Editor's picks. The three "Editor's picks" on the homepage rotate monthly and are selected from casinos currently performing well on the rubric. Commission rate doesn't determine the selection, but a casino without an affiliate program wouldn't appear in this slot.

If you want to bypass the affiliate link

You can type any casino's name directly into your browser instead of clicking through. You'll get the same offer, the same experience. The site prefers that you don't, because affiliate commission is what keeps it running — but nothing forces the affiliate link. This line is in the disclosure because it's honest, not because it's trying to steer you.

Legal basis for this disclosure

Several jurisdictions require clear affiliate disclosure — including the US FTC, UK ASA guidance, and various EU consumer-protection regimes. This page and the persistent top bar on every page of the site exist to meet those standards plainly rather than burying the disclosure in a footer.

Challenging a score

If a casino operator or reader believes a score is unfair, the way in is new information. Email or Discord me — details at /about — and include what's changed. Re-reviews happen when operators materially change their offering and at least annually regardless.

What the affiliate links look like technically

sponsored nofollow tells search engines this is paid/affiliated content. noopener is a security best practice. target="_blank" opens the casino in a new tab so your place on this site is preserved.
