Methodology

How casinos on this site are scored.

Seven weighted criteria. Published scoring anchors. The same rubric applied to every casino, from the top of the list to the bottom — including the ones that pay the highest commission.

Version 1.0 · Published April 2026 · Last updated April 2026

The single biggest problem with casino affiliate review sites is ratings inflation. You'll read "review" pages on other sites where the worst casino on the list still gets 4.2 stars out of 5. Everything's "excellent." Everything's "top-rated." This is why nobody trusts affiliate sites, and it's why the rubric on this page exists.

Every casino is scored using the same seven-criterion rubric below. Scores are calculated from the sub-scores and commission rates are not part of the calculation. The sub-scores and the final number are published on every review page so you can see exactly how a score was put together.

Realistic score distribution: for any set of 40 casinos scored honestly, expect roughly 1–3 to land above 9.0, 6–10 between 7.5 and 8.9, 10–15 between 6.0 and 7.4, and a long tail of 10–15 below 6.0. If every casino on a site scores above 7.5, the rubric isn't real.

The seven criteria

Criterion Weight What it measures
Bonus fairness 25% Wagering requirement, max cashout from bonus, game contribution, bonus expiry, max bet during wagering.
Payout speed 20% E-wallet and bank transfer processing time, hidden daily/weekly caps, KYC sanity (once-only vs. every-withdrawal).
License tier 15% Player recourse and dispute resolution power — not whether the license is "legal" (they all are somewhere).
Game library & providers 15% Breadth, depth, top providers present (Hacksaw, Nolimit, Pragmatic, Play'n GO, Relax, NetEnt, Evolution), live dealer and crash-game availability.
UX & mobile 10% Load speed, mobile design quality, search, filters, account flows, cashier clarity. Tested in person.
Support 10% 24/7 live chat presence, English response time, response quality. Tested by actually starting a chat.
Trust signals 5% Brand age, parent company reputation, CasinoMeister/AskGamblers complaint record, responsible-gambling tools.

Anchors are the part that makes a score repeatable. Here's exactly what each sub-score means so that two different reviewers (or the same reviewer on two different days) would give the same casino the same number.

Game library — 15%

Algorithmic per casino: count of games, count of providers, whether top providers are present (Hacksaw, Nolimit, Pragmatic Play, Play'n GO, Relax, NetEnt, Evolution), live dealer availability, crash games. 9+ requires broad library plus live + crash. 5 is a small catalogue with few top providers.

UX & mobile — 10%

Hands-on test. Load speed via Google PageSpeed Insights, mobile design quality, search function, filter presence, cashier clarity (can you actually figure out how to deposit and withdraw without hunting?), registration flow. Not subjective — a real checklist is applied.

Support — 10%

Tested by contacting live chat with a real question. Measures: English availability, response time (under 2 minutes is good, under 30 seconds is excellent), whether the response actually answers the question or deflects. Support tested at different hours where possible.

Trust signals — 5%

Aggregated from CasinoMeister rating, AskGamblers complaint history, years operating under the current brand, presence of deposit limits, cooling-off, and self-exclusion tools.

Score calculation

Each sub-score (0–10) is multiplied by its weight, then summed and rounded to one decimal. Example: a casino scoring 8, 6, 10, 7, 7, 5, 8 on the seven criteria:

(8 × 0.25) + (6 × 0.20) + (10 × 0.15) + (7 × 0.15) + (7 × 0.10) + (5 × 0.10) + (8 × 0.05)
= 2.00 + 1.20 + 1.50 + 1.05 + 0.70 + 0.50 + 0.40 = 7.35

What doesn't factor in

Commission rate. Exclusivity of the affiliate deal. Whether the operator is "nice" to work with. Whether they buy advertising. All of these are commercial relationships — none of them belong in a player-facing score. If you ever see a score on this site that looks like it's been tilted by commercial interest, email or Discord me and I'll either justify it in writing on the review page or fix it.

Challenges and corrections

If a casino operator wants a score revised, the way in is new information — a published payout-time improvement, a removed restriction, a new license. Not commercial persuasion. Re-reviews happen when operators materially change their offering or at minimum annually.
